## 6:47 AM

We've been awake for an hour or two now. Barnacle sits next to me and stares at me. Begging for attention. He begins stretching(read scratching) on my leg. If I am not promptly getting everything ready for the day by now. He knows. He will insist. It will happen.

## The Breakfast Protocol

The cats have opinions about sequence:

1. Barnacle gets his wet food first or there will be a headbutt into the back of my knee
2. Bean watches the proceedings from the counter and judges everything
3. Fresh water must be poured even if the bowl is not empty — they know the difference somehow

## The Window

By 7 AM they are both at the window. There is a bird feeder outside that I put up specifically for this purpose. It is, without question, the best investment I have ever made. They communicate during this period using sounds I have never heard them make at any other time — a kind of chattering, clicking thing directed exclusively at birds.

## The Post-Breakfast Nap

Roughly 8 AM, both cats vanish. Bean goes under the bed. Barnacle takes the couch. I have not figured out what triggers the transition from window-watching to napping but it happens with clockwork precision every single morning.

## The Afternoon Reset

Around noon Barnacle reappears, fully rested and ready to be annoying again. The cycle continues.
