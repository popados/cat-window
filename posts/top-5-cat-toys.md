## The Criteria

Not all cat toys survive contact with actual cats. After going through an embarrassing number of purchases, the ranking method is simple: did the cats use it more than once, and is it still physically intact after two weeks?

## The List

### 1. Da Bird Feather Wand

Nothing tops it. Both cats go completely feral for this wand in a way that no other toy has matched. Barnacle launches off furniture. Bean does her little chirp-and-pounce maneuver. Five stars, no notes.

### 2. Crinkle Tunnel

Cheap, loud, and they both love hiding in it. Bean uses it as an ambush spot and the crinkle sound keeps them investigating it independently even when nobody is playing with them.

### 3. Mylar Balls

Basically free, nearly indestructible, and perfect for batting under furniture at three in the morning. Barnacle is especially devoted to these.

### 4. Cat Dancer Wire Toy

A bent wire with cardboard bits on the end — looks absurd, works perfectly. The unpredictable movement drives them crazy in the best way.

### 5. Hexbug Nano

The only electronic toy that made the list. It vibrates and moves erratically which is catnip for Bean specifically. Barnacle is skeptical and watches from a distance like it owes him money.

## Honorable Mention

A wadded-up receipt. Free. Universally beloved. Non-negotiable second place if we are being honest.
